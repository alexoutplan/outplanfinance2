The Cost of Doing Nothing

Every tax year that passes
without a plan is money
you can’t get back.

Inaction isn’t neutral. It’s a decision — and it has a price.

Pension Allowance

Up to £180,000 of tax-efficient space — most business owners never use it.

The annual pension allowance is £60,000. Miss this year and it’s gone. But with carry forward, you can reclaim up to three years of unused allowances — contributing up to £180,000 in a single payment.

Employer pension contributions made through your limited company are fully deductible against corporation tax. That means every pound you put into your pension also reduces your company’s tax bill.

£60,000 lost per year unplanned
Profit Extraction

Taking a salary and dividends without a plan is one of the most costly mistakes a business owner makes.

Most directors default to a salary and take dividends without ever questioning whether the split is right, the timing is optimal, or whether there are more tax-efficient ways to extract value from their company.

Getting this right means understanding your personal allowance, your dividend allowance, your marginal tax rate, and how your extraction strategy interacts with your pension contributions, your spouse’s position, and your company’s corporation tax liability. Done well, it can save thousands of pounds each year — not through avoidance, but simply by using the allowances and structures that already exist.

Thousands lost each year without the right plan
ISA Allowance

£20,000 of tax-free investment space disappears every 5th April.

Your ISA allowance is £20,000 per person, per year. Once the tax year ends, that allowance is gone permanently — it cannot be carried forward.

A couple could be sheltering £40,000 a year from tax on growth and income. The longer this goes unused, the greater the compounding opportunity you’re leaving on the table.

£20,000 per person lost every April

These aren’t complicated problems. They’re missed opportunities. And the longer they go unaddressed, the more they cost.
